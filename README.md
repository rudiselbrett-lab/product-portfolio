## How I work

**Forward-deployed, then systematized.** At Ally, I embedded directly with business lines to understand their operational problems before writing a single requirement. Once patterns emerged, I standardized them into reusable implementation patterns that cut onboarding time in half and let 3x more teams ship without additional engineering support.

**Governance is a product feature.** In regulated environments, risk and compliance aren't obstacles — they're requirements. I treat AI governance (guardrails, LLM observability, MRM review) the way I treat any other product requirement: scope it, build it, measure it. 100% platform traffic coverage with guardrails isn't a compliance checkbox — it's what makes the platform trustworthy enough to scale.

**Portfolio discipline over backlog growth.** More AI use cases isn't better. I ran portfolio operating reviews at Ally that screened out ~30% of low-value initiatives and concentrated investment on the 13+ strategic products representing 80%+ of portfolio value. Saying no to good ideas to protect great ones is real product work.

**Adoption is the product.** Shipping is table stakes. At every company I've worked at, the harder problem was getting people to actually use what we built and change how they work. I design for adoption from day one: feedback loops, embedded support, and success metrics that reflect real behavior change, not just feature launches.
