Adoption Turnaround: Getting Teams to Actually Use an Enterprise AI Platform

Ally Financial · AI Product Manager · 2024–Present

9% → 21%
Adoption among eligible employees
9 → 43
Use cases in production
3x
More teams shipping without engineering

The Problem

The platform existed, but adoption sat around 9%. The capability was there; the path to using it wasn't. Teams couldn't get from idea to production without central engineering, so most gave up or never started.

The Product

A rebuilt intake-to-production pipeline plus a forward-deployed model, where my team embeds with a business line and builds the first use case alongside them. Behavioral instrumentation underneath showed exactly where teams dropped off.

My Role

I treated adoption as a product problem, not a marketing one. I used behavioral data to find where teams actually got stuck, fixed the friction that was costing us rather than the friction that was easy to fix, and let what I learned embedding with business lines drive the roadmap.

Why It Matters

Adoption is the metric that proves a platform is worth having. Doubling it while tripling the teams who could ship on their own is leverage: the platform got more valuable without my team becoming the bottleneck for every use case.

Agentic AI for Audit: A Zero-to-One Product in Six Weeks

PricewaterhouseCoopers · Associate Product Manager · 2023–2024

6 weeks
Concept to working MVP
1,000
Auditors in the pilot
120,000+
Practitioners on the platform it joined

The Problem

Auditors spent enormous time reading documents by hand to surface findings worth a closer look. It was slow, inconsistent person to person, and didn't scale as volume grew. The judgment was real human work; the reading in front of it was the bottleneck.

The Product

An agentic system that ingested and parsed documents, surfaced likely findings, and handed them to a person to review instead of deciding on its own. The machine did the first pass; the auditor made the call. The real product was the handoff: getting the human-in-the-loop step right so people trusted what came back.

My Role

I was part of a 20-person pilot team that took this from idea to MVP in six weeks, contributing on the product side alongside a senior PM. The lesson that stuck: the point was never to automate the auditor away, it was to design the seam between machine and human so the machine earned trust one handoff at a time.

Why It Matters

Zero-to-one AI work with no clean spec, a new capability, and a six-week clock. That it ran with 1,000 auditors and then folded into the next-generation platform serving a practice of 120,000+ is the proof it held up outside a demo.

Safe by Default: Making AI Shippable in a Regulated Bank

Ally Financial · AI Product Manager · 2024–Present

100%
Platform traffic covered by guardrails and observability
10 / 4
Agents classified across four risk tiers
Inherited
How teams get safety — not bolted on

The Problem

In a regulated bank, AI doesn't reach production without controls, and controls usually get added at the end — which slows teams down or pushes them to avoid AI entirely. Every team was reinventing its own guardrails or steering clear. There was no shared foundation anyone trusted.

The Product

A governed foundation teams inherit instead of rebuild: enterprise guardrails, observability across 100% of platform traffic, and an agent classification framework that sorts agents by privilege, data sensitivity, and ownership so the right controls apply automatically. Safety became part of the platform, not a gate at the end.

My Role

I defined what "safe by default" needed to look like from the builder's side, then worked with our risk and model risk partners — inside their frameworks rather than around them — to make the compliant path the easy one. The goal: a team gets safety just by building on the platform, not by surviving a review.

Why It Matters

Trust is what scale depends on. You can't put AI in front of people in a regulated setting until the controls are real, and you can't ship dozens of use cases if each needs its own safety review. Making safety infrastructure is what let the portfolio grow without the risk growing with it.
