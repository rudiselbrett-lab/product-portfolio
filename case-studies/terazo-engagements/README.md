# Multi-Client Product Delivery — Terazo

**Company:** Terazo  
**Role:** Engagement Lead / Product Manager  
**Scope:** 7+ concurrent client engagements, $3M+ in revenue  
**Industries:** Retail, healthcare, logistics, SaaS  
**Timeline:** March 2021–June 2023

---

## Context

Terazo is a software consultancy. As Engagement Lead and PM, I was responsible for the full product lifecycle across multiple simultaneous client engagements — from early discovery through shipped software. No two clients had the same problem, the same technical context, or the same definition of success.

This role compressed a lot of PM reps into a short period. Seven concurrent engagements means seven different stakeholder environments, seven different technical teams, and seven different sets of tradeoffs happening at the same time.

---

## What I owned

**Discovery and customer research.** Each engagement started with ambiguity. Clients came with symptoms, not diagnoses — "our operations team wastes too much time" or "we need an app for this." My job was to get underneath those statements and identify what was actually worth building. I ran stakeholder interviews, workflow observations, and problem framing workshops before any requirements were written.

**Product strategy and roadmap.** Translated discovery findings into a product direction the client could fund, engineering could execute, and end users would adopt. In a consulting context, the roadmap also had to fit within contract scope — which meant ruthless prioritization from day one.

**Delivery ownership.** Managed backlog, sprint ceremonies, and cross-functional teams across the full build cycle. On most engagements, I was the connective tissue between client stakeholders, engineering, and design — translating in both directions.

**Relationship management.** $3M+ in revenue across concurrent engagements required trusted senior relationships on each client account. I was accountable for client satisfaction, scope management, and renewal conversations alongside delivery.

---

## Representative work

**Retail client:** Built a custom inventory operations tool that replaced a manual, spreadsheet-driven process. Discovery revealed the actual bottleneck wasn't data visibility (what the client asked for) but decision latency — store managers had the data and didn't know what to act on. The product surfaced recommendations, not just reports.

**Healthcare client:** Delivered a workflow automation tool for a clinical operations team. Compliance constraints shaped the product from day one — data handling, audit trails, and role-based access weren't features we added at the end, they were requirements that structured the architecture.

**Logistics client:** Ran discovery for a real-time tracking product where the stated problem ("we need a dashboard") masked a more interesting one (field teams weren't looking at dashboards; they needed push notifications at key decision points). Pivoted the product direction based on user research before development started.

---

## What this taught me

**Ambiguity is the default, not the exception.** Across seven concurrent engagements, I never once had a client who handed me a clear, correct problem statement. The ability to run structured discovery and arrive at a defensible product direction — fast — is the core PM skill in a consulting context.

**Adoption is harder than delivery.** Shipping software is table stakes. Getting a client's team to change how they work is the real deliverable. I learned to design for adoption from day one: feedback loops, change management hooks, and success metrics that reflect behavioral change, not just feature launches.

**Senior relationships and product quality reinforce each other.** The clients who trusted me most gave me the access to do better discovery. Better discovery produced better products. Better products renewed contracts. The flywheel runs in both directions.
