Value

Where I create the most leverage

Turning ambiguity into executable strategy

I go into undefined, high-stakes problems, find the real leverage, and turn it into clear bets a team can run with. Less thrash, and a defensible point of view on where to place the money.

Opportunity discovery grounded in the business

I find high-value problems by getting close to the people who have them, and I kill low-value work early so capacity goes where it matters. The result is a focused set of capabilities that compound, not a pile of shallow pilots.

Platforms and operating models that scale beyond me

I solve a problem once and make it reusable — whether that's a capability, a governance lifecycle, or an operating model that lets many teams build without a central bottleneck. Leverage that grows without headcount growing with it.

Owning the hard tradeoffs, especially speed versus risk

The decisions that matter sit between functions: innovation versus control, central versus federated. I own those, and in regulated settings I've made the safe path the fast path instead of treating governance as a tax.

Translating between strategy and execution

I can talk architecture with engineering, then give leadership a clear, outcome-focused narrative that drives a decision. The payoff is alignment: fewer things lost in translation and faster calls.
